Chipper Jones tips his cap to fans at Turner Field.

Notable Achievements

  • 1990: Drafted #1 overall by Atlanta Braves in MLB Amateur Draft
  • 1993: Made MLB debut with Atlanta Braves
  • 1995: Finished second in National League Rookie of the Year voting
  • 1999: Won National League Most Valuable Player
  • 2006: Played for Team USA in inaugural World Baseball Classic
  • 2008: Won National League batting title with .364 batting average
  • 2012: Retired from MLB after 19 year career, all with the Atlanta Braves
  • 2018: Elected to National Baseball Hall of Fame in first year of eligibility
